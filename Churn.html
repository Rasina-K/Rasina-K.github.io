Bank Customer Churn

Project Synopsis

The primary goal of the Bank Customer Churn Prediction project is to examine the demographic and financial details of bank customers. This analysis includes factors such as age, gender, credit score, country, account balance, and more. The primary aim is to forecast whether a customer is inclined to terminate their association with the bank. The phenomenon of customer churn, where customers opt to discontinue their relationship with the bank, has significant implications for the institution's business and profitability.

The dataset encompasses various independent variables, representing potential factors that might impact a customer's decision to terminate their association with the bank. These variables encompass customer-specific details such as credit score, geographical location, age, tenure, bank balance, the number of bank products utilized, possession of a credit card, and the customer's active membership status with the bank.

Features

Column Name Description
RowNumber Row Number
CustomerId A unique alphanumeric identifier assigned to customer
Surname Customer's last name
CreditScore Numerical representation of an individual's creditworthiness
Geography Country of the customer
Gender Gender of the customer
Age Age of the customer
Tenure Number of years the customer has been with the bank
Balance Bank balance of the customer
NumOfProducts The number of bank products the customer is utilizing
HasCrCard Binary flag for whether the customer holds a credit card with the bank or not
IsActiveMember Binary flag for whether the customer is an active member with the bank or not
EstimatedSalary Estimated salary of the customer in Dollars
Exited Binary flag 1 if the customer closed account with bank and 0 if the customer is retained
